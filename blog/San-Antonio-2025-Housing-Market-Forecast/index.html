San Antonio Housing Market Forecast 2025: What Buyers Need to Know

June 7, 2025 6 min read

San Antonio's real estate market continues to be one of Texas' most dynamic in 2025, with shifting trends that every potential homebuyer should understand. Whether you're looking in Alamo Heights, Stone Oak, or the emerging neighborhoods on the Northwest Side, timing your purchase correctly could save you thousands.

Here's our expert analysis of the 2025 San Antonio housing market, including price trends, inventory forecasts, and strategic buying advice.

San Antonio Market Overview: 2025 Projections

San Antonio Home Price Growth Forecast

2020 2025 Projection

Expected 5.8% annual appreciation through 2025

According to recent data from the San Antonio Board of Realtors (SABOR):

  • Median home price: $325,000 (up from $295,000 in 2024)
  • Inventory levels: 2.1 months supply (still favoring sellers)
  • Days on market: Average 28 days (down from 35 in 2024)
  • New construction: 18% of total sales (concentrated in Northwest Side and Converse)

Key Market Drivers in 2025

Several factors are shaping San Antonio's real estate landscape this year:

Tech Job Growth Relocations Interest Rates

1. Economic Expansion: With companies like Tesla and Amazon expanding operations in Central Texas, San Antonio continues to attract skilled workers needing housing. The Medical Center area remains particularly strong for professionals.

2. Migration Patterns: 42% of current buyers are relocating from California, New York, and Florida—many bringing substantial equity that fuels competitive offers in popular areas like Alamo Heights and Stone Oak.

3. Interest Rate Environment: While rates have stabilized around 6.25% for 30-year fixed mortgages, any Federal Reserve cuts could trigger another surge in buyer demand.

Strategic Buying Tips for 2025

Winning Strategies in Today's Market

Based on our analysis of successful 2025 purchases:

1. Off-Peak Timing: November-February typically sees 12% less competition than spring months, with motivated sellers more open to concessions.

2. New Construction Alternatives: Many buyers overlook "like-new" homes (2-5 years old) that avoid the premium of brand-new builds but still offer modern layouts.

3. Expand Your Search: Emerging areas like Converse and Schertz offer better value while still being within 30 minutes of downtown San Antonio.
